# Use of Artificial Intelligence

Generative AI tools were used as editorial and programming support during preparation of the manuscript and this reproducibility package.

AI assistance was used for:

- drafting and refining documentation;
- organizing supplementary materials;
- checking consistency between manuscript claims and experimental artifacts;
- supporting code/documentation preparation.

AI assistance was not used as an autonomous scientific decision-maker. The authors remain responsible for the study design, experimental execution, data interpretation, statistical analysis, and final manuscript content.

Suggested confidential PeerJ staff note:

> AI-assisted tools were used to support language editing, code organization, documentation drafting, and reproducibility-package preparation. The tools included OpenAI ChatGPT/Codex through the OpenAI platform (https://openai.com/). The authors reviewed and validated all generated text, code, analyses, and interpretations. AI tools were not treated as authors and did not make independent scientific decisions.
